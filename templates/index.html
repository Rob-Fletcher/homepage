Experience

Research Assistant - ATLAS Experiment

University of Pennsylvania

Using some of the largest data sets in the world, I worked with teams of physicists in the search for new, lighter versions of the Higgs Boson. In the ATLAS datasets, signals are buried under large backgrounds so it is essential to model and understand these backgrounds well. I have worked on a new method for background modeling using Gaussian Process Regression to make a prediction on noisey data.

More about the ATLAS Experiment.
July 2012 - Present

Research Assistant - MICE Experiment

University of California, Riverside

As an undergraduate I worked on the Muon Ionization Cooling Experiment at the Rutherford Appleton Laboratory near Oxford, England. I studied the neutral particle content of the muon beam and presented my results at an international conference. I also spent time develping software and tools for the MAUS data analysis framework used by the experiment.
